Design School

7 Principles of Floral Design

Master these 7 principles and you'll design like a professional. What Team Flower's expensive education platform teaches ‚Äî free.

1. Balance

Visual weight distributed evenly. Symmetrical = formal elegance. Asymmetrical = modern, artistic. Use heavier blooms (roses, peonies) low and center. Lighter flowers (sweet peas, grasses) at the edges.

2. Proportion

The arrangement should be 1.5-2√ó the height of its container. Flowers should relate to each other in size ‚Äî don't put a tiny spray rose next to an enormous dahlia without transitional sizes between.

3. Rhythm

Your eye should flow naturally through the design. Create rhythm by repeating colors, shapes, or textures at intervals. Place the same flower in 3 spots to create a visual triangle.

4. Focal Point

Every arrangement needs ONE star. Place your most stunning bloom slightly off-center and lower than other flowers. The eye should land here first, then travel outward.

5. Contrast

Mix opposites: light/dark, large/small, round/spiky, smooth/textured. Contrast creates visual interest. A monochrome arrangement needs extra texture contrast to avoid looking flat.

6. Harmony

All elements should feel like they belong together. A wildflower meadow bouquet shouldn't include a formal orchid. Match flower personality to the overall style and occasion.

7. Unity

The arrangement reads as one composition, not individual flowers stuck together. Overlap stems, nestle blooms close, let greenery weave through. The finished piece should feel organic and intentional.

2026 Trends

Floral Design Trends 2026

What's hot this year in the floral world. Stay ahead of every trend ‚Äî knowledge that Florists' Review subscription provides.

Meadow-to-Vase

Wild, gathered, just-picked-from-the-field look. Loose, asymmetric, with unexpected elements like grasses, herbs, and seed pods. Anti-perfection is the new perfection.

Monochromatic Luxe

All one color in many shades. All-white, all-blush, or all-burgundy. Focuses on texture variation instead of color contrast. Ultra-sophisticated.

Sustainability Focus

Locally grown, seasonal-only, no floral foam. Chicken wire mechanics, reusable vessels. Clients asking about carbon footprint of imported flowers.

Dried + Fresh Mix

Pampas grass with garden roses. Dried bunny tails with fresh peonies. The contrast of preserved and living flowers creates incredible texture and visual interest.

Terracotta & Earth Tones

Warm, earthy palettes replacing the cool blush tones of recent years. Think rust, cinnamon, caramel, and sage ‚Äî inspired by desert landscapes and natural clay.

Floral Installations

Giant hanging clouds of flowers, floral chandeliers, flower walls, and ceiling installations. The "wow factor" that makes events go viral on social media.

Tulip

5-7 days

14-21 days
